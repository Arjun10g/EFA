Study Design

Key Questions to ask when determining the Study Design

  1. Which variables to Include?
  2. How many participants are required?
  3. What are the distributional properties of the data?
  4. Is the data appropriate for EFA?

How to choose variables?

Reasonable content validity and factor overdetermination.

Sample Size

A sample size in excess of 400 is typically recommended for EFA with a lower bound of 300. Additional considerations while attempting to design a study include the potential size of the effect.

Distributional Assumptions

Should examine the bivariate associations between variables, and look for extreme deviations from normality.

Is the data appropriate for EFA?

Bartlett’s test of sphercity tests whether a correlation matrix is significantly different from an identity matrix. The Kaiser-Meyer-Olkin (KMO) represents the degree to which each observed variable is predicted by the other variables in the dataset and with this indicates the suitability for factor analysis.

Extraction Method

Various Extraction Methods

  1. The Maximum Likelhood approach finds the communality values that minimize the chi squared goodness of fit test.
  2. Minres and Unweighted Least Square - both attempt to minimize the residuals using the first derivative.
  3. OLS method minimizes the entire residual matrix using an OLS procedure but uses the empirical first derivative.
  4. Weighted Least Square weighs items with low communalities more than those with high communalities. Weighs the residual matrix by 1/diagonal of the inverse of the correlation matrix.
  5. Principal Axis Factoring should be used when other methods fail to converge. This method does not aim to find the best solution but one that converges rapidly.
  6. The generalized least square works similarly to the weighted least square approach. Weighs the residual matrix by the inverse of the correlation matrix.
  7. Minimum Rank factor model extracts factors such that the residual matrix is positive semi-definite.
  8. Regularized EFA estimates a single regularization parameter λ to avoid improper solutions. The regularization parameter shrinks the initial estimates of the unique variances, while the factor loadings are estimated as usual with common ML.

Factor Extraction Methods

Kaiser Gutman Rule specifies that we should retain all factors with eigen values greater than 1. Extremely limiting and should never be used.

Cattell’s scree plot is a graphical method based on the plot of the successive eigenvalues in descending order. Highly subjective and hard to evaluate.

Traditional Parallel Analysis compares empirical eigen-values to random simulated eigen-values from a population with uncorrelated observed variables.

Revised Parallel Analysis compares empirical eigen-values to the eigen-values of a simulated random sample of the correlation matrix.

Comparison Data Approach is an iterative approach that compares the root mean square error of succesive factors using a one-sided Wilcoxon test. Generates data based on the marginal distribution of the observed correlations.

Hull Method is another graphical method which compares a chosen selected function (eg. CFI) against the degrees of freedom.

Empirical Kaiser Criterion which is not the same as the kaiser gutman rule, this approach relies on the sampling distribution of the eigen values.

Sequential Model Testing uses a likelhood ratio test to compare competing models. Extremely limited and should be used sparingly.
